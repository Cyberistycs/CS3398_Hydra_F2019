What is Budgeting?

Budgeting is a way of prioritizing your spending through careful planning and organizing. This planning process lays out where all of your money goes. Budgeting can be accomplished by this general framework of three parts: money for expenses, money for saving, and money for everything else. Good budgeting is spending less than you are earning as you plan for your financial goals.

Reasons why you should budget your money

A budget helps you figure out your long-term goals and work towards them. A budget forces you to map out your goals, save your money, keep track of your progress, and make your dreams a reality. It ensures you don't spend money that you don't have. Building a budget forces you to take a close look at your spending habits. You may notice that you're spending money on things that you no longer use

3 Practical Budgeting Tips

1. Track All of Your Expenses

Track your spending on a daily basis so you know where all of your money is going.

2. Seperate Your Needs and Wants

Group your expenses into things that you must pay for and things that you can live without.

3. Create Realistic Goals

Decide what you want your finances to look like in the future.
